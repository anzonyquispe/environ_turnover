\section{Data and outcome construction}

\subsection{Pipeline}

Three production stages: (i) a Python data builder
(\texttt{C\_Programs/build\_panels.py}) that consumes the Stata
elections file plus a registry of outcome CSVs and writes
\texttt{analysis\_panel.dta} (one row per muni-election) and
\texttt{balance\_panel.dta} (balanced muni $\times$ year, 2001--2024);
(ii) two Stata do-files (\texttt{run\_rd.do}, \texttt{run\_didm.do})
that estimate the RD designs (via \texttt{rdrobust}) and the DCDH
switchers-in event study (via \texttt{did\_multiplegt\_dyn}); (iii)
this report, assembled by the \texttt{compile-landcover-report}
Claude skill.

\subsection{Outcomes ($K=9$)}

Seven IGBP MODIS land-cover layers (annual area shares in percentage
points, 2001--2024), one VCF tree-cover layer (MOD44B
\texttt{Percent\_Tree\_Cover}, 2000--2025), and one annual mean
nighttime-lights layer:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{forest} $= c_{01}+c_{02}+c_{03}+c_{04}$
  \item \texttt{mixed\_forest} $= c_{01}+\cdots+c_{05}$
  \item \texttt{shrublands} $= c_{06}+c_{07}$
  \item \texttt{savannas} $= c_{08}+c_{09}$
  \item \texttt{grassland} $= c_{10}$
  \item \texttt{agriculture} $= c_{12}$
  \item \texttt{crop\_nature} $= c_{14}$
  \item \texttt{tree\_cover} -- MOD44B v061 VCF
        \texttt{Percent\_Tree\_Cover}
  \item \texttt{mean\_night} -- annual mean nighttime-light radiance,
        2000--2024 (winsorised at the 3rd / 97th percentiles before
        $\Delta Y$).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sensor note for \texttt{mean\_night}.} The annual mean
roughly doubles between 2010 and 2014, reflecting the well-known
DMSP-OLS $\to$ VIIRS-DNB sensor transition. For the 2011 election
cohort the baseline is $Y_{2010}$ (DMSP era) and the post window is
$Y_{2012..2015}$ (mostly VIIRS), so $\Delta Y^{\text{main}}_{m,2011}$
inherits a mechanical positive jump. RD-2 (which includes the 2011
cohort) is affected; RD-1a / RD-1b (2015/19/23 only) are not.

\subsection{Marx--Pons--Rollet $\Delta Y$ construction}

For each (muni $m$, election year $t$),
\[
\Delta Y^{\text{main}}_{m,t}
  \;=\; \tfrac{1}{4}\sum_{\tau=1}^{4} Y_{m,t+\tau}
  \;-\; Y_{m,t-1},
\]
the post-election four-year mean minus the value in $t-1$. Shorter
horizons replace the $1/4$ post-mean by $1/k$ for $k\in\{1,2,3\}$;
the \texttt{pre3} robustness spec replaces $Y_{m,t-1}$ with the
mean of $Y_{m,t-3..t-1}$. The Python pipeline reproduces MPR's
Stata code (\texttt{2\_3\_build\_regdata.do}) to floating-point
precision: \texttt{winsor2 v, replace cuts(3 97)} via
\texttt{numpy.quantile(method='averaged\_inverted\_cdf')}, and a
single $(\mu_v, \sigma_v)$ per outcome derived from
$\Delta Y^{\text{main}}$ on the full election sample, applied to
every horizon variant and every sub-sample.

\subsection{Heterogeneity splits}

Sample splits for ``Above'' and ``Below'' use each outcome's own
\textbf{2010} baseline value. For outcome $v$, ``Above'' keeps the
municipalities with $Y_{m,2010}^{(v)}$ above the cross-muni median and
``Below'' keeps the rest. The split is therefore per-outcome (a
municipality may be ``above'' for forest and ``below'' for
agriculture).

\subsection{The three RD designs and their samples}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{RD-1a (incumbent-ideology change).} Score
  $\text{margin} \times \widetilde{\text{inc\_ideology\_win}}$.
  Treatment at the cutoff: winner's ideology differs from the previous
  incumbent's. Sample = 2015/2019/2023 only
  ($N=2,719$); \texttt{inc\_ideology\_win} is unobserved for
  2011 (no 2007-election record in this dataset).
\item \textbf{RD-1b (incumbent re-election).} Score
  $\text{margin} \times \widetilde{\text{inc\_won}}$.
  Treatment at the cutoff: previous incumbent did \emph{not} re-win
  (party-level turnover at a close incumbent-vs-challenger race).
  Sample = 2015/2019/2023 only ($N=3,279$); the raw
  \texttt{inc\_won} column is mechanically zero for every 2011
  winner.
\item \textbf{RD-2 (winner's ideology dichotomies).} Three
  transformations of the winner's ideology indicator; see
  Table~\ref{tab:ideo_transforms}. Sample =
  2011/2015/2019/2023 ($N=3,869$), dropping the small ``No info''
  ideology category.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{McCrary density test.} For each (design $\times$ outcome
$\times$ sample) we run \texttt{rddensity} (Cattaneo--Jansson--Ma) on
the design's running variable to test for manipulation at the cutoff.
The two RD-1 designs share a single running variable each
(\texttt{score\_inc} and \texttt{score\_inc\_won}), and the three
RD-2 transformations use \texttt{score\_l\_vs\_rc},
\texttt{score\_lc\_vs\_r}, \texttt{score\_lr\_vs\_c} respectively.
The $p$-values from the standard binomial / Wald-like test are reported
in a per-outcome footer; throughout, $p_{q}<0.10$ is flagged with a
warning.

\section{RD-1a: incumbent-ideology change}

Score: $\text{score}_{\text{inc}} = \text{margin}\times\widetilde{\text{inc\_ideology\_win}}$,
where $\widetilde{\text{inc\_ideology\_win}} = +1$ if the winner's
ideology differs from the previous incumbent's and $-1$ if it matches.
Treatment at the cutoff: ``ideology changed''. Estimator:
\texttt{rdrobust} with $p=1$, triangular kernel, MSE-optimal bandwidth
(\texttt{bwselect("mserd")}), SEs clustered at the municipality. Each
cell reports the bias-corrected RD coefficient paired with the robust
SE / $p$-value (the ``Robust'' line of \texttt{rdrobust}).

\section{RD-1b: incumbent re-election}

Score: $\text{score}_{\text{inc\_won}} = \text{margin}\times\widetilde{\text{inc\_won}}$,
where $\widetilde{\text{inc\_won}} = +1$ if the previous incumbent did
\emph{not} re-win (turnover) and $-1$ if the incumbent re-won.
Treatment at the cutoff: ``turnover'' (incumbent lost a close election).
This is the canonical close-elections RD on turnover \`a la
Marx--Pons--Rollet. Larger sample than RD-1a because \texttt{inc\_won}
is observed for all non-2011 winners regardless of the ideology
comparison.
